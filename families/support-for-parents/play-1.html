Play

Support for parents and babies to support development through play.

Why is play important?

Playing helps your little one's brain to develop. It's also amazing for their language and communication skills!

A simple game of peek-a-boo, shaking a rattle or singing a song is much more important than passing time. The learning from this is incredible! Children learn to communicate, develop motor skills and it helps with problem-solving. Even stacking and knocking over bricks helps with maths and science!

So playing is the best way to start learning.

Tummy Time

Tummy time is a great activity to develop your baby’s physical abilities & promote emotional bonds. It will help them to roll, sit up, crawl and eventually walk.
