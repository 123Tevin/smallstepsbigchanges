Volunteer with SSBC

Our amazing volunteers help deliver SSBC services and activities in their communities.

Who are SSBC volunteers?

Our volunteers are so important to us. 

  • They are parents from our communities.
  • They are passionate about improving access to SSBC services.
  • They help us design services that people in our communities want and need

How can I volunteer for SSBC?

Giving a small amount of time each week will make a big difference to our services and families who use them

There are three main ways to volunteer for SSBC:

  • Lend a helping hand at SSBC groups
  • Sign up to be an SSBC Parent Ambassador
  • Become a Read On Nottingham Literacy Champion
  • Use your voice to respond to SSBC surveys and questionnaires. 

 

What are the benefits of volunteering?

  • Meet other local parents
  • Access training and gain new skills
  • Make a real difference to families in your area

Enquire about being a Parent Ambassador

Could you be a Parent Ambassador and help SSBC reach more parents? Help us design services, attend events, review applications for funding, and much more.

There’s no fixed time commitment. We provide all the materials, training and support that you need. For many of the activities, we can provide free crèche places for your little one.

Are you an SSBC Family?

We provide services to families in Nottingham who live in the areas of Aspley, Bulwell, Hyson Green & Arboretum and St Ann’s. Check if you’re in an SSBC area.

Enter your postcode like this: NG1 3FB

Are you an SSBC Family?
You do not live in an SSBC area. We prioritise our groups and services for families in SSBC areas. If you are looking at SSBC groups, you can contact the Family Mentor hub closest to you to find out if they have spare spaces.
